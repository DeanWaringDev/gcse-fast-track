# Screen 1: Understanding Fractions

A fraction is a way of showing **part of a whole**. Think of sharing a pizza!

## What Do The Numbers Mean?

Every fraction has two numbers:

```
  3  â† Numerator (top number) - How many pieces you have
  â€”
  4  â† Denominator (bottom number) - How many pieces in total
```

So **3/4** means "3 pieces out of 4 pieces total" - you've got 3 slices of a pizza that was cut into 4 slices!

## Real-World Examples

You see fractions everywhere:

- **Cooking:** Add 1/2 cup of flour
- **Time:** It's quarter past three (1/4 of an hour = 15 minutes)
- **Money:** That costs Â£1.50 (1 pound and 50/100 or 1/2 pound)
- **Sport:** We're 3/4 of the way through the match

## Key Point ğŸ”‘

The **denominator** (bottom number) tells you what size the pieces are. The bigger the denominator, the SMALLER each piece!

Think about it:
- 1/2 of a pizza = BIG slice (cut into 2 pieces)
- 1/8 of a pizza = tiny slice (cut into 8 pieces)

## Proper vs Improper Fractions

**Proper fraction:** Top smaller than bottom (like 3/4)
**Improper fraction:** Top bigger than bottom (like 7/4) - you've got MORE than one whole!

# Screen 5: Finding Common Denominators
