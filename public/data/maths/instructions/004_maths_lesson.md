# Screen 1: What Are Decimals?

**Decimals** are another way of writing numbers, especially when we need to show parts that are **smaller than 1**.

## Place Value with Decimals

Just like whole numbers have place values (units, tens, hundreds), decimals also have place values:

- 4 hundredths = 0.04 (which is 4/100)

**Total:** 10 + 2 + 0.3 + 0.04 = **12.34**

## Key Point

The further right you go from the decimal point, the **smaller** the value:
- Tenths (0.1) are bigger than hundredths (0.01)
- Hundredths (0.01) are bigger than thousandths (0.001)

Think of it like this:
- 0.5 = 5 tenths = half
- 0.05 = 5 hundredths = much smaller!

# Screen 2: Reading and Writing Decimals

Being able to **read** and **write** decimals correctly is essential!

## How to Read Decimals

**Example: 3.7**
Say: "Three **point** seven" (not "three point seventy")

**Example: 12.45**
Say: "Twelve **point** four five" (not "twelve point forty-five")

**Example: 0.8**
Say: "Zero point eight" or "Nought point eight"

## Writing Decimals in Words

**0.3** = three tenths
**0.07** = seven hundredths  
**0.25** = twenty-five hundredths
**2.5** = two and five tenths
